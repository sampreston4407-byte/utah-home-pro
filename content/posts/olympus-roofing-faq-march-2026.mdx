# Top Questions Salt Lake County Homeowners Ask About Roofing

If you've ever stood in your driveway squinting at your roof wondering whether that dark patch is a problem, you're not alone. Roofing questions come up constantly for Utah homeowners — and the answers matter because the wrong decision can turn a $400 repair into a $10,000 project. Here are the questions we hear most often.

## How much does a roof replacement cost in Salt Lake City?

Most homeowners in Salt Lake County pay between $9,000 and $12,000 for a standard asphalt shingle replacement on a typical 2,000 sq ft home. What moves that number: roof pitch, square footage, whether a full tear-off is required (Utah code allows max two shingle layers), material tier, and decking condition. Get at least three written quotes before committing.

## Do I need a permit for a roof replacement in Salt Lake County?

Yes. Most cities in Salt Lake County require a permit for re-roofing. A licensed contractor should handle this on your behalf. If a contractor tells you permits aren't necessary, that's a red flag — unpermitted work can flag your home at resale and may void manufacturer warranties.

## How do I know if I need a repair or a full replacement?

Repairs make sense when damage is isolated — a few missing shingles, a single flashing leak, or minor storm damage in one area. Full replacement becomes necessary when more than 30% of shingles are damaged or curling, when there are multiple separate leak points, or when the decking shows water damage or rot.

## What causes most roof damage in Utah?

Hail is the leading cause of insurance claims along the Wasatch Front. After that: ice damming (water backing up under shingles during freeze/thaw cycles), UV degradation from intense summer sun, and wind damage from canyon storms. Ice dam prevention through proper attic ventilation and insulation is consistently underinvested in by Utah homeowners.

## How do I verify a roofer is licensed in Utah?

Go to the Utah Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing website at dopl.utah.gov and search the contractor's name or company. It takes about 30 seconds. Out-of-state crews roll through after every hailstorm — always verify before signing anything.

## What roofing certifications should I look for?

GAF Master Elite and Owens Corning Preferred Contractor are the two most meaningful manufacturer certifications. Fewer than 3% of roofing companies nationally hold either one. These certifications unlock 50-year material warranties that unqualified contractors simply cannot offer.

## What should a good roofing estimate include?

A solid estimate should itemize materials, labor, tear-off costs, permit fees, and the disposal of old materials. Be skeptical of vague lump-sum quotes with no line items — that's how surprise charges appear after the job starts. A trustworthy contractor gives you detail before you sign, not after.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long does a roof last in Utah?**
Standard asphalt shingles last 15–25 years in Utah. The Wasatch Front's combination of UV exposure, hail, heavy snow, and freeze/thaw cycles accelerates wear significantly compared to milder climates. Metal roofing lasts 40–70 years and is growing in popularity across Salt Lake County.

**What is ice dam removal?**
Ice dams are ridges of ice that form at roof edges, trapping meltwater that backs up under shingles and leaks into the home. Professional removal uses steam equipment to safely clear ice without damaging the roof surface. Heat cable installation prevents recurrence in problem areas.

**How do I find a good roofer in Salt Lake County?**
Start by verifying the license on dopl.utah.gov, then check Google and BBB reviews, ask for local references from the past 12 months, and confirm they pull permits. Any contractor who skips permits, won't provide local references, or pressures you to sign same-day is worth avoiding.
