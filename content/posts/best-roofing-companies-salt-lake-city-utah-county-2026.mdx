## What to Look for in a Utah Roofer

Utah's climate is uniquely hard on roofs. Intense summer UV, heavy snow, ice damming, hail, and dramatic freeze/thaw cycles mean your roofer needs to understand local conditions — not just be cheap. Before hiring anyone, verify their license on the Utah DOPL website, confirm they pull permits (most don't and it can flag your home at resale), and make sure they're actually local. Out-of-state crews roll through after every hailstorm and are gone before warranty issues come up.

## How Much Does a Roof Replacement Cost in Salt Lake City and Utah County?

Most homeowners in the Salt Lake City and Utah County area pay between $9,000 and $12,000 for a standard asphalt shingle replacement on a typical 2,000 sq ft home. What moves that number:

- **Roof pitch** — steeper roofs require more safety equipment and labor time
- **Square footage** — larger homes cost more
- **Tear-off** — Utah code allows a maximum of two shingle layers. If you're already at two, a full tear-off is required, which adds cost
- **Material tier** — standard architectural shingles vs premium or impact-resistant vs metal roofing
- **Decking condition** — if the underlying OSB is rotted or damaged it needs replacing before new shingles go down

Labor rates for skilled roofing in Utah average around $45/hour. Get at least three quotes and be skeptical of anything significantly below the others.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a permit for a roof replacement in Utah County or Salt Lake County?**
Yes. Most Utah cities require a permit for re-roofing projects. A licensed contractor should handle the permit process on your behalf. If a contractor tells you permits aren't necessary, that's a red flag — unpermitted work can flag your property at resale and may void your warranty.

**How do I verify a roofer is licensed in Utah?**
Go to the Utah Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing (DOPL) website at dopl.utah.gov and search the contractor's name or company. Takes about 30 seconds and is the single most important check you can do before signing anything.

**How long does a roof last in Utah?**
Standard asphalt shingles typically last 15–25 years in Utah. The Wasatch Front's combination of UV exposure, heavy snow, hail, and freeze/thaw cycles accelerates wear compared to milder climates. Metal roofing can last 40–70 years. Impact-resistant shingles are worth considering if you're in a hail-prone area.

**What causes most roof damage in Utah?**
Hail is the leading cause of insurance claims. After that: ice damming (water backing up under shingles during freeze/thaw cycles), UV degradation from intense summer sun, and wind damage from canyon storms. Ice dam prevention through proper attic ventilation and insulation is something most Utah homeowners underinvest in.
