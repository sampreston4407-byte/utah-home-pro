# What Utah Homeowners Should Know About Their Roof

Your roof is one of the most expensive components of your home — and one of the most overlooked. In Utah, where temperatures swing from 100°F summers to sub-zero winters and hailstorms roll through without warning, understanding the basics of roofing can save you thousands.

## How Long Does a Roof Last in Utah?

Most asphalt shingle roofs last 20–30 years under normal conditions. In Utah Valley and Salt Lake County, UV exposure significantly accelerates wear. If your roof is over 15 years old, it's worth a professional inspection even if you don't see obvious damage.

Metal roofing lasts considerably longer — 40–70 years — and is becoming more popular along the Wasatch Front for homeowners who want a long-term solution and don't want to think about their roof again.

## Signs You Need a Repair vs. a Full Replacement

Repairs make sense when damage is isolated — a few missing shingles, a flashing leak around a chimney, or minor storm damage in one area. Full replacement becomes necessary when:

- More than 30% of shingles are damaged or curling
- You're seeing granule loss collecting in your gutters
- There are multiple separate leak points
- The decking underneath has water damage or rot

A useful rule of thumb: if a repair costs more than 50% of what a replacement would cost, replace.

## What to Ask Any Roofing Contractor

Before signing anything, ask these questions:

- Are you licensed in Utah and carrying liability insurance?
- Do you pull permits for replacement work?
- What's the warranty on both materials and labor?
- Can you provide local references from the past 12 months?

Unlicensed contractors are especially common after hailstorms in the Salt Lake area — out-of-state crews roll through and are gone before warranty issues surface. Always verify a contractor's status on the Utah Division of Occupational and Professional Licensing (DOPL) website at dopl.utah.gov before signing anything.

## How Much Does a Roof Replacement Cost in Utah?

Most homeowners in Salt Lake County and Utah County pay between $9,000 and $12,000 for a standard asphalt shingle replacement on a typical 2,000 sq ft home. What moves that number:

- **Roof pitch** — steeper roofs require more safety equipment and labor time
- **Tear-off requirements** — Utah code allows a maximum of two shingle layers; if you're already at two, a full tear-off adds cost
- **Material tier** — standard vs premium vs impact-resistant shingles vs metal
- **Decking condition** — rotted or damaged OSB needs replacing before new shingles go down

Get at least three written quotes and be skeptical of anything significantly below market — it usually means shortcuts somewhere.

## The Most Common Roofing Mistake Utah Homeowners Make

Waiting. A small leak that costs $300 to fix today can turn into $8,000 in decking, insulation, and drywall damage within a single winter. Ice damming — water backing up under shingles during freeze/thaw cycles — is a major culprit in Utah and often starts as something that looks minor from the outside.

Annual inspections, especially after hail season in the spring and before winter sets in, are the best money you can spend on home maintenance.
